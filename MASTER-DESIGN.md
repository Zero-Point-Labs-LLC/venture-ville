# 🌆 Venture Ville — Master Design Document

**Version:** vCurrent  
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-22  
**Status:** Core systems defined, ready for turn loop design

## 🎯 Core Game Fantasy

Players run competing startups. They:
- **Build capabilities** → **Ship products** → **Scale platforms** → **Win via Users + Money**

Victory pressure comes from:
- 👥 **Users** → momentum / positioning / influence
- 💰 **Money** → scaling power / late-game dominance

**Experience target:** A startup race with asymmetric engines and strategic tempo control.

---

## ⏱️ Target Game Pacing

Derived from Catan benchmarking.

- **Ideal length:** ~20–30 turns per player
- **Early** → capability divergence
- **Mid** → convergence + friction
- **Late** → platform dominance race

This pacing informs:
- Funding cadence
- Engine timing
- Node density
- Victory thresholds

---

## 🌳 Roadmap Tree System

Each company has a unique **roadmap tree** representing product bets and strategic milestones.

### Nodes
Require combinations of **Resources** and generate:
- 👥 **Users**
- 💰 **Money**
- 🎴 **Progress Cards**

### Tree Philosophy
- Multiple viable starting nodes
- Parallel branches
- Convergence into:
  - Flagship products
  - Platforms
  - Ecosystems

### Design Goals
- Early strategic divergence
- Midgame convergence tension
- Late-game platform control

---

## 🧱 Resource System (Inputs)

Resources represent what a startup produces or accumulates.

- ⚙️ **Engineering** — infrastructure / technical leverage
- ✏️ **Design** — usability / differentiation / creation
- 🧭 **Product** — convergence / monetization logic
- 📣 **Marketing** — growth / distribution
- 👥 **Hiring** — organizational expansion

Resources are spent to:
- Unlock roadmap nodes
- Build Talent pieces
- Trigger scaling actions

---

## ⭐ Talent System (Execution Layer)

**Teaching model:**  
Resources are what your startup produces.  
Talent is how your startup grows and executes.

### Talent Pieces
- **Partner** — distribution leverage (road analog)
- **Pod** — cross-functional team (settlement analog)
- **Team** — scaled org unit (city analog)
- **Manager** — efficiency / scaling buffer
- **Contractor** — temporary burst capacity
- **Influencer** — brand amplification

**Progression fantasy:** Partner → Pod → Team

---

## 🧱 Organizational Capacity Build (City Walls Equivalent)

### ⭐ Reorg (Reorganization)

A non-productive build that increases headcount / team capacity.

- Does **not** generate resources
- Does **not** accelerate upgrades
- Acts as a **scaling safety buffer**

**Strategic role:**
- Early game: optional
- Mid/late game: critical

Failure to build may cause:
- Inefficiency
- Layoffs / attrition
- Capacity penalties

Creates tension between: **Growth vs Stability**

---

## 🎴 Progress Card System

Cities & Knights–inspired tactical decks.

### How Cards Are Earned
- Players roll **discipline dice**
- Matching progress card drawn
- Advancement tracks act as **global thresholds** (not card gates)

### Deck Identities
- 🔴 **Engineering** — sabotage / denial / reversals
- 🟢 **Design** — information / swaps / perception pressure
- 🔵 **Product** — tempo / extra actions / coordination
- 🟣 **Marketing** — hype spikes / market theft
- 🟠 **Recruiting** — human capital volatility

**Card philosophy:**  
Play → impact → move on  
Short text, swingy, reactive, minimal bookkeeping.

## 📈 Advancement Track Hierarchies

Three parallel domains inspired by Cities & Knights.

### 🧭 Product Track
**Market Fit → Moat → Monopoly**
- Validation
- Defensibility
- Market control

### ⚙️ Engineering Track

- Technical discovery

## 🌩️ Market Event System (7 Roll Replacement)

### Core Mechanic
Rolling a 7 triggers a **Market Event** + advances **Time**.

This replaces robber/barbarian punishment.

### Event Distribution (Proposed)
- 50% **Month End** (baseline time pressure)
- 25% **Tailwinds** (opportunity spikes)
- 25% **Headwinds** (strategic setbacks)

---

## ⏰ Individual Funding Pressure

When **Time** advances:

Players must meet funding requirements or choose consequences:
- Downgrade builds
- Freeze upgrade slots
- Layoffs / capacity loss

Creates:
- Individualized risk
- Runway realism
- Macro pacing engine

## 🚨 Structural Design Issues

### Issue #1: Economic Slope of Shipping

**Current problem:**  
Prototypes and Apps do not sufficiently improve:
- Resource generation
- Tempo
- Efficiency
- Board leverage

**Fix direction:**
- **Prototypes** → economy shapers (conversions, discounts, probability control)
- **Apps** → economic engines (recurring income, multipliers, network effects)

**Shipping must increase a player's economic slope.**

---

### Issue #2: Unique Trees vs System Simplicity

**Risk:**  
Fully unique upgrade rules may:
- Increase teach time
- Create balance explosion
- Fragment system identity

**Proposed solution:**  
**Unique Trees using Shared Upgrade Archetypes**

Companies differ via:
- Ordering
- Triggers
- Branching
- Theme

**Not** via unique rule systems.

- Hiring enables liquidity / execution
- Product is convergence glue
- Late game should reward platform control
- Engine timing parity across companies is critical
