# Creative Constraints: Original Turn Loop Design

**Priority:** CRITICAL  
**Date:** 2026-03-22  
**Directive:** BE BOLD. INNOVATE. DON'T COPY.

---

## ⚠️ IMPORTANT: Research vs Innovation

**Research other games to understand principles, NOT to copy mechanics.**

### What Research Is For:
- ✅ Understanding what makes turn structures work
- ✅ Learning pacing principles
- ✅ Identifying common pitfalls
- ✅ Extracting design patterns (not specific mechanics)

### What Research Is NOT For:
- ❌ Finding a template to copy
- ❌ "Catan but with startups"
- ❌ "Wingspan meets Terraforming Mars"
- ❌ Safe, derivative design

---

## 🎨 Creative Challenge

**Design 3 turn loop proposals that:**

1. **Start from first principles** - What does a startup race NEED?
2. **Question assumptions** - Does it need dice? Turns? Phases?
3. **Think thematically first** - What mechanic captures startup reality?
4. **Embrace asymmetry** - Different turn structures for different phases?
5. **Take risks** - Better to be interesting than safe

---

## 🚫 Forbidden Patterns (Too Derivative)

### ❌ "Roll Dice, Collect Resources, Build, Pass"
This is Catan. We already have Catan. Don't make another Catan.

### ❌ "Action Selection from Limited Pool"
This is every Euro game. It's fine, but not unique.

### ❌ "Everyone Does Phase X, Then Phase Y"
This is Race for the Galaxy / Puerto Rico. It works, but it's been done.

### ❌ "Play Cards from Hand for Actions"
This is fine as a component, but not as the core loop.

## ✨ Creative Prompts

### Thematic First Principles

**What does it FEEL like to run a startup?**
- ⚡ **Speed vs Planning** - Move fast or move smart?
- 🎲 **Controlled Chaos** - Predictable strategy meets market volatility
- ⏰ **Runway Pressure** - Time is always ticking
- 🔄 **Pivot or Persevere** - Strategic flexibility required
- 📈 **Exponential Growth** - Small advantages compound
- 🤝 **Network Effects** - Being first matters (but not everything)

**How can the turn structure embody these?**

## 💡 Unconventional Ideas to Explore

### Asymmetric Turn Structure
What if different companies have different turn flows?
- Social media startup = fast turns, more volatility
- Enterprise SaaS = slower turns, more planning
- Hardware startup = front-loaded investment, delayed payoff

### Real-Time Elements
What if some decisions happen simultaneously?
- Everyone plans their quarter, then reveals
- Auction for turn order each round
- Real-time market that shifts as players act

### Event-Driven Structure
What if there are no "turns" - only events?
- Market Event deck drives the game forward
- Players react to events as they happen
- Turn order emerges from event resolution

### Momentum System
What if speed compounds?
- Early movers get momentum tokens
- Momentum lets you take faster/bigger actions
- But momentum can crash (market corrections)

### Funding-Driven Phases
What if the game structure follows funding rounds?
- Seed phase = different rules than Series C
- Each funding stage unlocks new actions
- Game accelerates as you scale

### Competitive Bidding
What if players bid for position each round?
- Spend resources to act first
- Or save resources and act later with more info
- Creates strategic tension on timing

### Asynchronous Actions
What if players don't wait for turns?
- When you complete an action, you draw next action card
- Everyone plays simultaneously until event triggers
- Fast players get more actions, but risk overextension

## 🎯 The "What If?" Method

**For each proposal, start with a bold "What if?"**

### Example:
> **What if there were no dice?**
> → How would resource production work?
> → Cards? Market deck? Fixed tracks?
> → What creates variability then?

> **What if players took turns simultaneously?**
> → How do conflicts resolve?
> → What prevents analysis paralysis?
> → How does interactivity work?

> **What if the game board itself changed each turn?**
> → Market tiles flip/shift
> → Resources move around
> → Spatial positioning becomes dynamic

> **What if faster players got more actions but more risk?**
> → Speed vs caution trade-off
> → Momentum mechanics
> → Crash and burn possibility

## 🔬 Test Your Proposals Against These

### Is it thematically resonant?
Could this turn structure exist in a different theme? If yes, it might be too generic.

### Is it memorable?
Will players remember this game for its unique turn structure? Or will it blur with others?

### Does it create tough decisions?
Are there interesting trade-offs baked into the core loop?

### Is it elegant?
Complex is fine. Complicated is not. Can you explain it clearly?

### Would you want to play it?
Honestly - does this sound FUN? Exciting? Worth 90 minutes?

## 🎨 3 Proposal Archetypes (NOT TEMPLATES)

### Proposal 1: THEME-FIRST
**Start with:** "What mechanic best captures startup chaos?"
**Constraint:** Must feel unmistakably like a startup, even if complex
**Risk tolerance:** High - bold ideas welcome

### Proposal 2: MECHANICS-FIRST  
**Start with:** "What creates the most interesting decisions?"
**Constraint:** Must be elegantly simple despite depth
**Risk tolerance:** Medium - clever but learnable

### Proposal 3: HYBRID INNOVATION
**Start with:** "What's never been done before that could work here?"
**Constraint:** Must be unique, even if it requires new design patterns
**Risk tolerance:** Very high - swing for the fences

## 🚀 Permission to Fail Boldly

**This is the ideation phase. You have permission to:**
- ✅ Propose something that might not work
- ✅ Suggest mechanics that need testing
- ✅ Break conventions
- ✅ Make Len go "wait, what? ...but that could be amazing"

**Better to propose something interesting that needs refinement than something safe that's boring.**

---

## 🎯 Success Criteria

**You'll know a proposal is good if:**

1. **You can't point to another game and say "like that but..."**
2. **It makes thematic sense for startups specifically**
3. **It creates moments you're excited to playtest**
4. **It has a signature mechanic people will remember**
5. **Len reads it and thinks "I've never seen that before"**

---

## 📝 Output Format

For each proposal, include:

### The Hook
One sentence that captures the unique idea.
> "What if the market itself drove turn order through bidding?"

### The Why
Why this mechanic captures startup reality.
> "Startups compete for timing - launching first matters, but so does timing the market."

### The How
The actual turn structure, step by step.

### The Risk
What could go wrong? What needs testing?

### The Upside
If this works, what makes it special?
