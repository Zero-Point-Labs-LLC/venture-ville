# Advancement Track Hierarchies

**Status:** ✅ Product & Engineering locked, ⚠️ Design TBD  
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-22  
**Dependencies:** None (parallel global progression)

---

## Problem Statement

The game needs:
- **Global progression** — Shared advancement beyond individual companies
- **Competitive thresholds** — Race for strategic milestones
- **Thematic depth** — Meaningful hierarchies, not arbitrary tiers
- **Mechanical rewards** — Tangible benefits for advancement

---

## Solution: Three Parallel Advancement Domains

Inspired by Cities & Knights, these tracks represent **industry-wide progress** that benefits all players unevenly.

### Track Philosophy

Each track follows: **Discovery → Defensibility → Control**

Players contribute to tracks via specific actions (TBD), and reaching thresholds unlocks abilities or Progress Card improvements.

---

## 🧭 Product Track (Locked)

### Hierarchy: Market Fit → Moat → Monopoly

| Level | Name | Theme | Unlocks |
|-------|------|-------|---------|
| 1 | **Market Fit** | Validation | Basic Product cards |
| 2 | **Moat** | Defensibility | Enhanced Product cards |
| 3 | **Monopoly** | Market Control | Dominant Product cards |

**Strategic Meaning:**
- **Market Fit** — You've proven demand exists
- **Moat** — You've built sustainable competitive advantage
- **Monopoly** — You control the market

**Mechanical Role:**
- Progress on this track may improve Product Progress Card quality
- May unlock special node types in Roadmap Trees
- May affect User generation efficiency

## Advancement Mechanics

### How Players Advance Tracks (Proposed)

**Option 1: Spending Resources**
- Spend specific resource combinations to move up tracks
- Creates strategic tension (advance track vs build nodes)

**Option 2: Node Unlocks**
- Certain Roadmap nodes grant track progress
- Ties company development to global advancement

**Option 3: Milestone Achievements**
- First to reach X Users → advance Product track
- First to build Y Teams → advance Engineering track

**Decision:** TBD based on turn structure design

## Strategic Intent

### Competition vs Cooperation

Tracks should create **asymmetric benefits**:
- Everyone can advance, but not all players benefit equally
- Some companies may specialize in certain tracks
- Late-game track positions may differentiate strategies

### Timing Pressure

- Falling behind may lock you out of critical abilities
- Racing ahead may waste resources better spent elsewhere
- Balanced advancement may be optimal (but boring)

---

## Alternatives Considered

### Rejected: Single Unified Track
**Why not:** Loses thematic richness, reduces strategic variety

### Rejected: Company-Specific Tracks
**Why not:** No competitive race, less tension

### Rejected: Locked Gates (Must Hit Level X to Advance)
**Why not:** Creates hard blockages, frustrating catch-up

## Risks & Concerns

⚠️ **Runaway leader** — First to advance may snowball too hard  
⚠️ **Meaningless progression** — If rewards are weak, why bother?  
⚠️ **Complexity bloat** — 3 parallel tracks may overwhelm new players  
⚠️ **Strategic opacity** — Players may not understand when to advance vs build
